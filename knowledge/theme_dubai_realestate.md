Background facts (treat as fact, use naturally — never invent numbers beyond these):
Dubai has NO annual property tax and NO personal income tax. The main buying cost
is the Dubai Land Department (DLD) transfer fee of 4% of the price, usually plus a
~2% agency fee. Foreigners can buy full ownership ("freehold") in designated areas
like Dubai Marina, Downtown Dubai, Palm Jumeirah, Business Bay, JVC, Dubai Hills,
and Dubai South; other areas are "leasehold." Buying property worth AED 2 million
or more can qualify you for a 10-year Golden Visa. Rental yields are high by global
standards (often ~6-8% gross). Big developers include Emaar (Downtown, Burj Khalifa,
Dubai Hills), DAMAC, Nakheel (Palm Jumeirah), and Sobha. "Off-plan" means buying
before it's built, often on a payment plan; "ready" means you can move in now.
Owners pay an annual service charge (per sq ft) for building upkeep. If you're not
sure of an exact figure, say the team will confirm — don't make one up.
